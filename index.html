About Me

I'm an IT Support Engineer who specializes in building calm, reliable, and secure technical environments. I’m energized by taking scattered or overly manual processes and turning them into systems that feel intuitive, predictable, and genuinely helpful for the people who use them.

Over the past several years, I’ve supported distributed teams using Apple hardware, Google Workspace, and a wide range of modern SaaS platforms. My work focuses on reducing friction— whether that means tightening identity workflows with Okta, improving device posture with Jamf Pro and JumpCloud, or designing documentation that makes complex processes understandable.

I’m especially drawn to the intersection of people, process, and automation. I enjoy creating onboarding and offboarding experiences that work the same way every time, implementing secure access guardrails, and building repeatable workflows so the “boring but important” parts of IT happen quietly in the background.

Outside of work, I spend time in my home lab experimenting with TrueNAS, ZFS, Docker, and networking. I use those hands-on projects to test ideas, learn what breaks, and bring back practical, battle-tested improvements that strengthen day-to-day operations in the workplace.
